About the Park

Flathead Lake National Park, where you can climb mountains, swim lengths, hike trails, learn about nature, and pretty much anything to do with nature. As the nations newest park, we take pride in how we do things. So come on. Journey into the untamed wilderness and stake your claim in the brand new history of this park. Because as we said earlier, this place is our home, and we want it to be yours too.

Our History

The idea of Flathead Lake National Park was created in 2018 when people saw the beautiful scenery and the 2.5 million acres of National Park spce waiting for them and teeming wildlife on the spot of a national forest south of Glacier National Park. The push to get the park recgonized as a national park began and picked up steam with support from presidential canidates like Bernie Sanders, Ben Carson, and Marco Rubio. After surveying the area, Environmental Protection Agency officials said the park should be recgonized. President Donald Trump signed the executive order on May 2, 2018, and the park came into being.
Today, the park brings in people of all professions all over the world. Visitors can explore the beautiful scenery and wilderness in the park as they hike the numerous trails, fish in the countless lakes and rivers, camp out and backpack at camping sites, and even climb mountains. Nature lovers will want to visit the Flathead Lake Nature Preserve, licensed through the national park service. Bird watchers will be astounded at all the different birds and species. Make sure you check it out as your adventure is waiting for you at Flathead Lake National Park and Flathead Lake Nature Preserve.
Whether you come to get away from the mess or to seek out a new adventure, Flathead Lake National Park is your place. It's just waiting for you to decide when. So make sure to come, no matter what you do, and we will be waiting for your next adventure.

Our History

The idea of Flathead Lake National Park was created in 2016 when people saw the beautiful scenery and teeming wildlife on the spot of a national forest south of Glacier National Park. The push to get the park recgonized as a national park began and picked up steam with support from presidential canidates like Bernie Sanders, Ben Carson, and Marco Rubio. After surveying the area, Environmental Protection Agency officials said the park should be recgonized. President Donald Trump signed the executive order on June 30, 2018, and the park came into being.
Today, the park brings in people of all professions all over the world. Visitors can explore the beautiful scenery and wilderness in the park as they hike the numerous trails, fish in the countless lakes and rivers, camp out and backpack at camping sites, and even climb mountains. Nature lovers will want to visit the Flathead Lake Nature Preserve, licensed through the national park service. Bird watchers will be astounded at all the different birds and species. Make sure you check it out as your adventure is waiting for you at Flathead Lake National Park and Flathead Lake Nature Preserve.
